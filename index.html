Astronauts Data Exploration

Using the CORGIS JSON dataset

Dataset Overview

The Astronauts dataset from the CORGIS Data Library contains detailed information about astronauts, their missions, vehicles, and lifetime statistics. It combines public data from NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, and other space agencies into an easily accessable format for research.

Research Questions

  • What types of missions do astronauts perform?
  • Which spacecrafts or vehicles are ,ost commonly used?
  • Who are some of the most significant astronauts in space history?
  • How have gender, nationality, and experience levels changed over time?

Research Findings

Astronauts take part in a variety of missions including scientific research, satellite repairs, and spacewalks. Over Time, the vehicles used have evolved from the Vostok and Apollo programs to the Space Shuttle, Soyuz, Dragon, and Artemis missions.

Some of the most influential astronauts include Yuri Gagarin (First person in space), Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin (first moon landing), and Sally Ride (first American women in space).

This dataset provides opportunities to study how astronaut demographics and mission patterns have changed through time, showing trends in international collaboration and diversity in space exploration
